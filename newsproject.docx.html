The US and USSR on the Brink of Nuclear War!

October 22, 1962

President Kennedy Addresses the Nation from the Oval Office

The President of the United States addressed the Nation last night announcing that he had taken steps to respond to the Soviet buildup and installation of missiles in Cuba. The President Said that the crisis we are facing has been caused by the Soviet Union’s “secret, swift, and extraordinary buildup of Communist missiles” in the Western Hemisphere. The new missile sites are being deployed only 90 miles from American shores. The new medium range missiles have the capabilities of carrying nuclear war heads and reaching 1,000 nautical miles. Thus, the missiles could reach Washington D.C., or many other cities in the southeast parts of the United States.

Range of Soviet Missiles in Cuba

President Kennedy stated that Cuba has become a clear and present danger to the United States and most of the Western Hemisphere. Kennedy said that the move by the Soviets is unacceptable. Therefore, while he states that the US policy is to maintain a policy of patience and peace, it is necessary to defend our security. He outlined key steps that could lead to further escalation with the USSR and even a nuclear war.

First, President Kennedy authorized a strict naval quarantine and blockade for all weapons and military equipment being shipped to Cuba.

Second, the President authorized increased surveillance over the tiny island to ensure no further military build-up.

Third, the President made it clear that if a missile is launched from Cuba towards any nation in the Western Hemisphere, the US will retaliate with a military attack on the Soviet Union.

Last, the president demanded that Chairman Khrushchev dismantle and withdraw the Soviet missiles immediately.

The President asked our allies to be prepared in the event of conflict.  

In his closing remarks, the President warned the American people that the path forward is “full of hazards, but the cost of freedom is high, and Americans have always paid it.”

This was a grim reminder that the world is on the brink of nuclear war.

US Spy Plane Photographs Images of Soviet Missiles in Cuba!

October 17, 1962

       

Images of Soviet Missile Sites in Cuba

The Associated Press is reporting that sources at The White House have said a U.S. U-2 reconnaissance plane on a routine mission flying over Cuba has photographed what appears to be images of the Soviets building missile sites in the Cuban countryside. The photos were taken on October 14th.  The pilot of the aircraft, Major Richard Heyser is quoted as saying he is worried that he will be viewed as having started a war.

On October 15th, CIA analysts are said to have confirmed the information.  They spotted launchers, missiles and transport trucks which do indicate that the Soviets are building missile sites.  The missiles can carry nuclear warheads and can reach 1,000 nautical miles in distance. Thus, the missiles could easily reach targets across most of the eastern part of the United States including Washington D.C.

President Kennedy has called an emergency meeting with a team of his advisors to discuss how to handle this military threat.  Defense Secretary Robert Macnamara has said that the options range from a diplomatic resolution with Cuban leader Fidel Castro and Soviet premier Khruschev, a naval quarantine to stop the flow of the weapons to Cuba, all the way to a military attack to destroy the missile sites.  But this could cause an escalation.

President Kennedy is considering the options.

Khrushchev Backs Down! The USSR Dismantles Missiles!

October 28, 1962

USSR Premier Nikita Khruschev

Premier Khruschev has agreed to dismantle and remove the Soviet missiles from Cuba. According to White House sources, overnight, Attorney General Robert Kennedy met with the Soviet Ambassador Anatoly Dobrynin to work out the final details of the deal. On October 26th, Premier Khruschev sent a letter to President Kennedy asking him to work with him to de-escalate the conflict. Khruschev did not want to “doom the world to the catastrophe of thermonuclear war.” This was against the wishes of Fidel Castro who was urging an all-out-war.

Yesterday, October 27th, war appeared imminent as one of our U.S. U-2 planes was shot down, and Major Rudolf Anderson was killed.  But President Kennedy concluded that this was not ordered by Khruschev.  Later in the evening, Premier Khruschev sent another letter to Kennedy demanding that the U.S. remove its missiles from Turkey as part of the deal for the Soviets to dismantle their missiles in Cuba. Attorney General Bobby Kennedy met with Soviet Ambassador Anatoly Dobrynin saying that the U.S. planned to do so.

On October 28th, Khruschev wrote a letter to Kennedy agreeing that the Soviet missiles would be dismantled and removed from Cuba. This was the moment that the world could breathe a sigh of relief!
